With technology touching nearly every aspect of our society, it is essential that all students acquire skills and knowledge in computer science (CS). Unfortunately, availability of classes and interests from students is still limited due to a low presence of CS in K-12 education. To address this challenge, we have developed a system which uses hip hop music to teach basic computer programming skills.

Code Beats is offering a 1 week session in person summer camp which is open to rising 6th graders to rising 9th graders, at a cost of only $50 per student.

Students registered before April 1st have a discount of 50%, paying only $25. After April 1st, the price is full. For financial aid and scholarship, please contact codebeatsteam@gmail.com.

The payment must be done through Venmo to @CodeBeats, inform the student name in the payment. Students will receive a headset to use during the camp and can keep it after the last day of the camp.

There are four available camp sessions:

  • Camp 1 from June 13th to June 17th, 8am - noon

  • Camp 2 from June 13th to June 17th, 1pm - 5pm

  • Camp 3 from July 18th to July 22nd, 8am - noon

  • Camp 4 from July 18th to July 22nd, 1pm - 5pm


We will teach students to create their own hip hop beats using basic computer programming skills. The sessions will be conducted at VCU where students will be able to engage with professors from VCU’s College of Engineering and VCU's School of Arts, assistants and other students in the camp
